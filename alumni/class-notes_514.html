60's

Bruce Darnall '66 has written an article titled "Matt Holliday Leads Cardinals with Quiet Confidence On and Off the Field" on St. Louis Cardinal left fielder, Matt Holliday. It was published on-line by Athletes in Action, the sports ministry for Campus Crusade for Christ. It tells about his life and his Christian faith. The articles can be found at: www.athletesinaction.org/news then click on Pro Ministry.

80's

Jay Hein '87 has authored a new book on faith and service, The Quiet Revolution.

90's

Tracy Ahrens '92 has photography on display at the Kankakee Public Library, Third Floor View. Tracy’s photography reminds us to find peace in life with an invitation to lie down in the grass and contemplate the huge, wide open sky above. The photographs will be on display on the third floor until June 13.

Mellisa Thompson Holtzman '95 was among a select group of researchers and program representatives invited to the White House for a briefing hosted by the Task Force to Protect Students from Sexual Assault. The invitation was extended based on Mellisa's efforts (along with her colleague, Chad Menning) to develop, test, and refine a new sexual assault protection seminar, called Elemental. This seminar has been demonstrated to significantly increase students' self-protection awareness while simultaneously lowering incidences of assault. Few protection programs have produced similar results. Mellisa currently serves as the co-director and lead instructor for Elemental. The program originated at Ball State University, but is now offered at other colleges in the region as well (see www.elementalprotection.org).
