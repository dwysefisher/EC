Friday, April 10

6pm Golden "E" 50th Anniversary Dinner
Gammon Room, Melick Library

Members of the Class of 1966 will gather with their classmates for this event dedicated to 50 years of memories.

8:30–11pm MAROON (Mentoring Alumni Representing Outstanding Opportunity Networks) & GOLD (Graduates of the Last Decade) Launch Party.
Derer Lanes, 1464 US Highway 24 East, Eureka

Come out to the Eureka bowling alley back room for a casual evening of fun and catching up with old friends while welcoming the senior class of 2016 into the ranks of EC alumni. (All alumni & friends welcome)

Saturday, April 11

7–7:40am Run with Reagan Registration
Alumni Tent, Corner of Burgess Street and Reagan Drive

The Alumni Office presents "Run With Reagan," an exciting and healthy way to kick-off the Saturday morning of Alumni Weekend! Proceeds bene- fit the Ronald W. Reagan Society of Eureka College and the Alzheimer's Association. Cost is $30 and includes race entry, t-shirt and post-race pancake breakfast.

8:30am Run with Reagan 5K race begins

8–9:30am Pancake Breakfast & Race Award Ceremonies
Dickinson Commons on College Avenue

All-you-can-eat pancakes and sausage, followed by Run with Reagan recognitions. Complimentary for race participants, $5.50 per person for non-participants.

9:30am Class of 2013 Walkway Dedication
Corner of College Ave. and Burgess St.

9:45am Dedication of James Jacob House & Toliver Tree
204 E. Campus, near Sanders Hall

10:30am The Labyrinth Experience.
Learn the secrets of the Labyrinth with Chaplain Bruce Fowlkes and students from Dr. Thurwanger's freshmen seminar class who have focused on the care and understanding of the college labyrinth this year. This session will feature special guest, Warren Lynn, Executive Minister for Christian Vocations for the Christian Church (Disciples of Christ), and an advanced labyrinth facilitator providing leadership in vocational discernment, self-care, spiritu- al practice and wellness. The Labyrinth near East Reagan Drive

12pm Experience Eureka Luncheon
Terrill Room, Cerf Center Around the World: Exploring Earth's Other 95% with National Geographic Expeditions. Eureka College Trustee, Flo Banwart and her husband, retired Caterpillar vice president Sid Banwart, will share sites and experiences from their "round-the-world" expeditions with The National Geographic Society. Flo and Sid will share stories, answer questions and explore the other 95% of the world's population who do not live in the United States. Buffet lunch: $12 per person.
