Welcome New Students

Congratulations on your selection of Eureka as your undergraduate college. In Greek, Eureka means, “I have found it” and at Eureka, you will find many new friends, outstanding faculty and staff, and amazing opportunities for learning inside and outside the classroom. Your participation in Jump Start will prepare you well for your undergraduate experience at EC.

Again, I personally welcome you to campus and the best of wishes on your high school graduation. I look forward to seeing all of you on campus in the fall!

Yours ‘neath the Elms at Eureka College,
J. David Arnold, Ph.D.
President

Day One

  • 10–12 pm: Cheerleading Practice for Tryouts (Optional) Reagan Gym
  • 12–1 pm: Lunch (On your own, many great local spots) List will be provided
  • 1–3 pm: Check-In Cerf Center, Burgoo
    Students will receive their orientation packet, completed paperwork will be collected, and student ID pictures will be taken.
  • 1–4 pm: Network ID, Email and Copy Cards Burgess 204
  • 1–4:40 pm: Individual meetings with Financial Aid and Business Office Financial Aid Office
  • 1–4 pm: Campus Tours (Optional) Cerf Center
    Tours will leave at the top of every hour from Moser Lobby.
  • 1–5 pm: Eureka College Bookstore Open Cerf Center
  • 2–3 pm: Foreign Language Placement Test Burgess 101
    Students who took Spanish or French in high school are encouraged to take the placement test.
  • 3:30–4:30 pm: English Placement Burgess 101
  • 5–5:30 pm: Welcome and Meet Your FYE Leaders (Everyone) North Terrill Room
    Orientation officially begins here. This session highlights important information about your two-day experience.
  • 5:30–6:30 pm: Ice Breakers and Teambuilders (Students) South Terrill
    Get to know your fellow classmates and current E.C. students in a fun and interactive way!
  • 5:30–6:30 pm: Letting Go (Parents) Gammon Room
    This session deals with the feeling of loss parents might experience with watching their children matriculate into college and adulthood. FERPA rights and Title IX: Sexual Misconduct will be discussed.
  • 6:45–7:40 pm: Dinner w/ First Year Seminar Class (Students) Dickinson Commons
  • 6:45–7:40 pm: Dinner w/ Professional Staff (Parents) Dickinson Commons
  • 8 pm: Floor Activities Various Residence Halls

Day Two

  • 8–8:45 am: Breakfast Dickinson Commons
  • 8:15–8:30 am: Honors Program Meeting (Invitation Only) Owen’s Conference Room, Dickinson Commons
  • 8–8:45 am: Individual meetings with Financial Aid & Business Office Financial Aid Office
  • 8–8:45 am: Network ID, Email and Copy Cards Burgess 204
  • 9 am–4 pm: Eureka College Bookstore Open
  • 9–10 am: “College, let it begin” (Everyone) North Terrill Room
    FYE Leader skit following the lives of students throughout their first year in college and offering a unique parent perspective.
  • 10–10:30 am: Academic Overview (Everyone) North Terrill Room
  • 10:30 am–12:30 pm: Advising (Group A Students) Melick Library/various locations
  • 10:30 am–12:30 pm: FYE Q&A (Group A Parents) North Terrill
  • 10:30 am–12:30 pm: Campus Engagement (All Group B) Burgess/various locations
    Professional staff members from the Student Programs & Services Department and the Learning Center will be available to discuss opportunities at Eureka College and how to best utilize each resource: Leadership & Service, Residence Life & Safety, Career Planning, and Academic Support.
  • 12:30–2 pm: Red Devil Pride Lunch Dickinson Commons
    Student athletes and parents will sit with their designated team. Not a Student Athlete, no problem! Sit with one of our designated tables to learn how you can show your EC Spirit: EC Band, Dance Team, Cheerleading, Campus Activities Board & Student Senate.
  • 2–4 pm: Advising (Group B Students) Melick Library/various locations
  • 2–4 pm: Student Q&A (Group B Parents) North Terrill
  • 2–4 pm: Campus Engagement (All Group A) Burgess/various locations
    Professional staff members from the Student Programs & Services Department and the Learning Center will be available to discuss opportunities at Eureka College and how to best utilize each resource: Leadership & Service, Residence Life & Safety, Career Planning, and Academic Support.
  • 4:30 pm: Cheer Team Tryouts (Optional) Reagan Gym

All guests should be checked out of the residence halls by 4:30 pm.
