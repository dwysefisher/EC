First Year Student

If you are applying as a first time, please:
  1. Fully complete the application for admission.
  2. Print the Secondary School Report/Transcript Request and give to your high school counselor to complete and return WITH official transcripts from your high school. Official transcripts must be sent directly from your high school to Eureka College.
  3. Request/Submit ACT and/or SAT results to Eureka College if your score is not on your official high school transcript. (Our ACT code is 1022; our SAT code is 1206)
  4. Print the Application Agreement Statement that appears after you submit the application. This confirmation page can also be printed by logging into the What's Next Illinois website.
Note: Applications are considered on a rolling basis following receipt of all required items. All supporting materials should be mailed to:
Eureka College Office of Admissions 300 E. College Avenue Eureka, IL 61530

Transfer Student

If you are applying as a transfer student with 30 semester hours or more of college credit, please:
  1. Fully complete the application for admission.
  2. Request official transcripts from all colleges and universities be sent to Eureka College.
  3. If you have less than 30 semester hours of college credit, please Request official high school transcripts and ACT/SAT scores.
  4. Print the Application Agreement Statement that appears after you submit the application. This confirmation page can also be printed by logging into the What's Next Illinois website.
Note: Applications are considered on a rolling basis following receipt of all required items. All supporting materials should be mailed to:

[accordion-item title="International Student"]

International Student

If you are applying as an international student, (in addition to the requirements described above for Freshmen and Transfer) please:
  1. Fully complete the application for admission.
  2. Submit TOEFL results, if your first language is not English.
  3. Request official transcripts, in English, from all colleges and universities be sent to Eureka College.
  4. Provide a Statement of Finance.
  5. Print the Application Agreement Statement that appears after you submit the application. This confirmation page can also be printed by logging into the What's Next Illinois website.

Notes for International Students

  • Eureka College does not charge an application fee.
  • The Secondary School Report is attached to the application form. If you apply online, this form must be printed from the website. Copies of all secondary school and (if applicable all college/university) transcripts in English.
  • A minimum Grade Point Average (GPA) on a 4 point scale is required.
  • Students from non-English speaking countries should submit ACT or SAT scores or have a minimum score of 550 on the Paper-Based Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) or CBT equivalent. Attendance at an American School or High School generally will mean the TOEFL is waived as long as classes in English are of a "B" grade or higher. In addition to all normal application procedures, international students must demonstrate the ability to supply funds to finance their education. International students may be considered for all Eureka scholarships.
  • A completed copy of the Sources of Support Form.
Note: Applications are considered on a rolling basis following receipt of all required items. All supporting materials should be mailed to:
Eureka College Office of Admissions 300 E. College Avenue Eureka, IL 61530
The Office of Admission will notify you of the receipt of your application with a reminder of outstanding materials needed before an admissions decision can be made.
