Be a part of the Summer Arts Festival at idyllic Eureka College!

The Eureka College Summer Arts Festival is happy to announce a new day camp for young string players, grades 5 through 8, with an emphasis on fundamentals of tone production, rhythm, intonation, and ensemble playing. An outstanding faculty of highly-gifted performing teachers join David Commanday to create five fun, challenging days that will really kick-start your playing!

Resident campers stay in double rooms in the new, state-of-the-art Ivy Residence Hall on the Eureka campus.

Faculty

David Commanday, cellist, Director-Conductor and Artistic Director of the Heartland Festival Orchestra. Named Illinois Professional Conductor of the Year 2011, he is also Conductor of the Young People's Symphonic Orchestra of Webster University's Community Music School in St. Louis. Kristin Witzig Schmidt, violin David Getz, viola, violin Peter Martin, viola, violin Aaron Kaplan, cello

Tuition

5-Day Residency: $490 (Includes 3 meals, snacks, Ivy Hall residence dorms) All-Day Student: (10am–2:30pm) $375 (Includes lunch, dinner, snacks) *Limited need and/or merit scholarships available to qualified students.
