Thursday, May 12

Senior Party

The Senior Party will be held on the Spirit of Peoria Riverboat in Peoria, IL. Reservations are required and must be made by 4pm on Tuesday, April 26th, 2016. Each senior receives a free ticket for themselves, and the cost for non-seniors is $8.00. Please make reservations on the attached Senior Party Ticket Request form. Buses will leave from in front of the Admissions Building on Burgess Street at 7:45pm and return at approximately midnight. (Note: Buses will not make any stops traveling to or from Peoria.) The cruise is scheduled from 9–11pm. All guests must board the Spirit of Peoria by 8:30pm. There will be food, a DJ, and a cash bar. Guidelines for Bus Transportation
  • Students and/or guests who are visibly intoxicated prior to departure will not be allowed to ride the bus. This is up to the discretion of SPS Staff.
  • No alcohol will be permitted on the bus. Be prepared for bag/purse searches and pocket checks.
  • Only sealed non-alcohol beverages will be allowed in the bus. All opened beverages will be disposed of prior to boarding the bus.
Guidelines for the Spirit of Peoria
  • Students and/or guests who are visibly intoxicated prior to departure will not be allowed to board the cruise ship and asked to return to campus via college vehicle.

* Students enrolling in Eureka College assume an obligation to conduct themselves in a manner compatible with the purpose and principles of the college. Student behavior should be such that concern for other persons is displayed. This principle is directly derived from the central purpose of the College. At a minimal level, this means that no conduct on the part of any individual or group should cause harm to others or seriously inconvenience others. At a more mature level, the principle of concern for others means thoughtful and helpful behavior towards other persons. The Basic Policy outlined in the Expectations and Standards in a community of Learning of the Student Handbook is central to the College's policy on student conduct and is considered an integral part of the Student Code of Conduct expected of all Eureka College Students.

Friday, May 13

Commencement Rehearsal

All Graduating Seniors are required to attend the commencement rehearsal at 2:00pm in Reagan Center Gymnasium. Individuals who cannot attend must receive exemption from the Office of Student Programs and Services prior to rehearsal. This rehearsal defines line-up for the Commencement processional, seating and marching across the stage.

Baccalaureate

Baccalaureate will be held in Becker Auditorium, but Seniors should line up in their caps and gowns outside the Chapel at 5:40pm. If bad weather, line-up will be in the Cerf Center. It is imperative that you be there on time for line-up. The service begins at 6:00pm.

Senior Dinner

Senior Dinner reservations are to be made on the attached Senior Dinner Ticket Request form. Each senior is allowed one free ticket for themselves, and 2 additional tickets at $16.00 each. Seating with other seniors may also be requested on the form and will be honored as space allows. Should additional tickets be desired, the Senior must request these by Tuesday, April 26th, 2016 at 4:00pm. On Tuesday, May 10th, the Office of Student Programs and Services will notify, via email, Seniors (who have previously reserved tickets) of any additional tickets for sale. If there are tickets available they will be sold on a first come, first serve basis. The dinner will be held in the Cerf Center Terrill Rooms at approximately 7:15pm. Doors will open at 7:00pm following Baccalaureate. There will be student volunteers available to assist with labeling and hanging your cap and gown. You must have a ticket to attend the dinner.
