Thursday, May 8

Senior Party

The Senior Party will be held on the Spirit of Peoria Riverboat in Peoria, IL. Reservations are required and must be made by 4:00pm on Tuesday, April 22nd, 2014. Each senior receives a free ticket for themselves; the cost for non-seniors is $8. Please make reservations on the attached Senior Party Ticket Request form. Buses will leave from in front of the Admissions Building on Burgess Street at 7:45pm and return at approximately midnight. (Note: Buses will not make any stops traveling to or from Peoria). The cruise is scheduled from 9:00–11pm. All guests must board the Spirit of Peoria by 8:30pm. There will be food, a DJ, and a cash bar. Guidelines for Bus Transportation
  • Students and/or guests who are visibly intoxicated prior to departure will not be allowed to ride the bus. This is up to the discretion of SPS Staff.
  • No alcohol will be permitted on the bus. Be prepared for bag/purse searches and pocket checks.
  • Only sealed non-alcohol beverages will be allowed in the bus. All opened beverages will be disposed of prior to boarding the bus.
Guidelines for the Spirit of Peoria
  • Students and/or guests who are visibly intoxicated prior to departure will not be allowed to board the cruise ship and asked to return to campus via college vehicle.

* Students enrolling in Eureka College assume an obligation to conduct themselves in a manner compatible with the purpose and principles of the college. Student behavior should be such that concern for other persons is displayed. This principle is directly derived from the central purpose of the College. At a minimal level, this means that no conduct on the part of any individual or group should cause harm to others or seriously inconvenience others. At a more mature level, the principle of concern for others means thoughtful and helpful behavior towards other persons. The Basic Policy outlined in the Expectations and Standards in a community of Learning of the Student Handbook is central to the College's policy on student conduct and is considered an integral part of the Student Code of Conduct expected of all Eureka College Students.

Friday, May 9

Commencement Rehearsal

All Graduating Seniors are required to attend the commencement rehearsal at 2pm in Reagan Center Gymnasium (Rinker Outdoor Theatre if perfect weather). Individuals who cannot attend must receive exemption from the Office of Student Programs and Services prior to rehearsal. This rehearsal defines line-up for the Commencement processional, seating and marching across the stage. If the weather is questionable, contact the Office of Student Programs and Services for the location of rehearsal.

Baccalaureate

Baccalaureate will be held in Becker Auditorium, but Seniors should line up in their caps and gowns outside the Chapel at 6:10pm. If bad weather, line-up will be in the Cerf Center. It is imperative that you be there on time for line-up. The service begins at 6:30pm.

Senior Dinner

Senior Dinner reservations are to be made on the attached Senior Dinner Ticket Request form. Each Senior is allowed one free ticket for themselves, and 2 additional tickets at $15 each. Seating with other Seniors may also be requested on the form and will be honored as space allows. Should additional tickets be desired, the Senior must request these by Tuesday, April 22nd, at 4pm.

On Tuesday, April 29th, the Office of Student Programs and Services will notify, via email, Seniors (who have previously reserved tickets) of any additional tickets for sale. If there are tickets available they will be sold on a first come, first serve basis.

The dinner will be held in the Cerf Center Terrill Rooms at 8:00pm. Doors will open at 7:30pm following Baccalaureate. There will be student volunteers available to assist with labeling and hanging your cap and gown. You must have a ticket to attend the dinner.

Saturday, May 10

Breakfast

A complimentary continental breakfast will be available in the Burgoo prior to Commencement from 8:30am to 9:30am. Come enjoy breakfast with your fellow graduates!

Commencement

Tickets Each Senior is initially limited to 6 tickets (graduate does not need a ticket). The Senior should designate the recipients of these tickets on the attached Commencement Reservation Request form. This list must be submitted to the Office of Student Programs and Services by Tuesday, April 22nd. Space is available on the form to allow the Senior to request additional tickets for family and friends. As space allows, the College will provide these additional tickets to Seniors. The Office of Student Programs and Services will notify Seniors via email of any increase in a student's ticket allotment. Professional Photography Graduates and family members will have the opportunity to receive professional photographs of the Commencement Ceremony. There will be two images taken of each graduate: receiving the diploma cover and a "smile-shot" with diploma cover after exiting the stage. Images will be available for via the Eureka College Alumni webpage. Special Accommodations If Commencement is in Reagan Gym, there will be handicapped parking in the Reagan parking lot. If special seating is required, please notify the Office of Student Programs and Services. Please notify your family that if Commencement is in Rinker Outdoor Theatre there will be reserved handicapped parking in the lot behind Pritchard Theatre if they should need to use it. Line-up Commencement line-up will begin at 9:45am on the Reagan Center Second Floor . It is imperative that you be there on time for line-up. (Line-up will be in front of the Chapel if Commencement is outdoors.) Start of Program The Commencement Ceremony begins at 10:30am. All individuals attending, except those participating in the ceremony, must have a ticket for admission if the event is indoors. The event will be held in Reagan Center Gymnasium and if the weather permits in Rinker Outdoor Theatre. President's Reception The President's Reception for graduates, families and guests will immediately follow the ceremony and will take place in the Terrill Rooms of the Cerf Center.

Cap and Gown/Announcement Information

Caps and gowns need to be picked up from the Bookstore before 3pm on Friday, May 9. Caps and gowns may be picked up during the last week of classes from 9am–3pm, Monday through Friday. All caps and gowns must be returned to the bookstore representative immediately following the Graduation ceremony. If you have any questions, please contact Jill Fehr at 309-467-6427.

Questions

Any questions regarding graduation activities for Seniors should be directed to the Ken Baxter at 309-467-6313.
