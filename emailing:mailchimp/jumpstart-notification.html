Preparing for Your First Course Registration

To have the best registration and advising experience possible, you can do some work ahead of time so that you arrive informed and having already considered some of the course choices you will have for your first year. Here are a few tips for a great advising and registration session:

  • Read the Academic Requirements and Policies section of the Eureka College Catalog. Here you'll find, among other important things, the course requirements of the General Education Curriculum. Some of these courses are required (like IDS101 and ENG103W), but in most of the Gen Ed curriculum you will have choices on which classes to take to meet requirements. Note where these choices are, and begin thinking about which courses you'd like to take. The Catalog also contains course descriptions, so read them carefully as you weigh your options.
  • Read the Program Description for your Major. The Catalog also contains program descriptions and course requirements for all the majors (and minors). If you've decided upon a major, examine its curriculum carefully. Reading the course descriptions and prerequisites, try to identify which courses are appropriate for first year students.
  • Transfer Credit? Have you taken any dual credit college courses? Summer course at a community college? Advanced Placement or CLEP exams? Be sure to send us your official transcripts or test score reports as soon as you can. Be sure to tell your advisor at Early Registration or Jump Start that you have transfer credit. You don't want to register for a class for which you already have credit!

Questions about degree requirements, transfer credit, advising, and other academic matters? Don't hesitate to call the Office of the Registrar at (309) 467-6896 or email the Office of the Registrar.

General questions about Early Registration Day can be directed to the Student Programs and Services Office at (309) 467-6420 or email Student Services.
