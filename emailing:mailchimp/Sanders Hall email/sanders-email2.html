At Eureka College, we believe your learning environment matters – in two ways

1. State-of-the-Art Facility – Sanders Hall, a new four-story classroom and laboratory building at Eureka College will include cutting-edge teaching space as well as contemporary student lounge areas, including:

  • Four new classrooms and a tiered multi-media classroom
  • Organic/inorganic chemistry lab
  • Student lounge space for collaborative learning

2. Global Sustainability – Sanders Hall is being built to qualify for LEED Certification*, the global standard for green building practices. The incredible environmentally friendly construction features include:

  • Geothermal power will heat and cool the building;
  • Locally manufactured and purchased building materials;
  • Native plants for landscaping that will also be used as teaching space;
  • And, even shower facilities for bike-riding commuters!

Sanders Hall is under construction now and will be open for classes this August!

Be among the first to learn within the walls of Eureka’s new classroom and laboratory building!

*LEED stands for Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design and is a program of the U.S. Green Building Council.
