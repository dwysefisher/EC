As we mourn the loss of Señora Julia Colorado, it is comforting to share in the memories that we all have of the lively, caring person who was such an integral part of the Eureka College community. As we move from mourning her loss to celebrating her life, may we move together as one community on this journey of healing.

Yesterday afternoon's campus gathering was a chance for alumni, faculty, staff, students and friends to share stories and laughter as we honored her memory. Via these stories we know that her love for this institution was abundant. And in this outpouring of support, it is clear that the love this institution has for her will continue to be felt throughout campus and with our alumni around the globe.

We encourage you to share your stories and memories of Julia with others. You can post your memories on our memorial Facebook page and take a moment to read the memories of others. You can also e-mail your stories to cchun@eureka.edu, who will compile them for Julia's family to enjoy.

Please continue to support and encourage each other through these challenging times. Utilize the resources we each offer and the support services available through the Student Programs and Services Office. It is the character of this special place to pull together in times of need.


Julia Colorado's Obituary

As found in today's Peoria Journal Star

EDWARDS - Julia Colorado, 56, of Edwards passed away at 8:57 p.m. on Sunday, Oct. 6, 2013, in Peoria.

Julia was born on March 31, 1957, in Peoria, the daughter of Scott and Suzanne Love Smith. She married Celestino Colorado on Aug. 9, 1980, in Peoria.

She is survived by her husband of 33 years; her daughter, Suzanne Elizabeth Colorado of Peoria; her mother, Suzanne of Peoria; two sisters, Elizabeth (Gary) Weber of Peoria and Alice (Keith) St. Clair of Grand Rapids, Mich.; three nieces; and two nephews.

Julia was preceded in death by her father.

She was an assistant professor of Spanish at Eureka College. She formerly taught at East Peoria Community High School.

Julia loved to write and read and had an international following for her articles online.

Cremation rites will be accorded. Memorial service will be at 6 p.m. on Friday Oct. 11, 2013, at Davison-Fulton Woolsey-Wilton Funeral Home. The Rev. Dr. Randall Saxon and Chaplain Bruce Fowlkes will officiate. Visitation will be two hours prior to services.

Memorials may be made to Eureka College International Student Program.

Please visit Julia's memorial website at www.woolsey-wilton.com.
