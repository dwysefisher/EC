My personal experience at Reagan Weekend was the most valuable opportunity that any college offered me to decide where would be the best place to spend the next four years of my life, and choosing Eureka College has hands down been one of the best decisions I have made for myself thus far!

Four years ago around this time, I accepted my invite to attend Reagan Weekend to compete for the Ronald W. Reagan Leadership Program. I remember not expecting much during the time leading up to it because I had not yet been convinced that Eureka College was where I wanted to go to college. However, after spending less than two days on campus during Reagan Weekend, I was exposed to a multitude of new opportunities and ideas which completely changed my view of college life. The chance to be a part of the campus community firsthand, stay in the dorms, already make plenty of new friends, and meet some of the most involved and caring faculty and staff, all helped me make the crucial decision that Eureka College was where I needed to be. 

Now as a second semester senior in college, I look back on all of the wonderful experiences I’ve had here, whether it was spending the summer in Haiti working in a medical clinic for one of my Reagan Program Mentorships, pulling all-nighters with friends to study for exams, or speaking alongside former Illinois Gov. Jim Edgar and president of the national Medal of Honor Society, Harold Fritz, during our annual Fall of the Berlin Wall ceremony. I’m so glad I picked Eureka College.
