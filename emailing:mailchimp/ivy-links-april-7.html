Students of Eureka College's Salve Regina Newman Center and Western Illinois University's St. Francis of Assisi Newman Center teamed up for a mission trip to Savannah, Ga., March 14–21. The trip was led by Father Eugene Radosevich of Eureka and Sister Janice Keenan and Leo Quinn, both of Macomb. The students helped Habitat for Humanity staff with its new ReStore location. They moved sale items, dismantled shelves and painted the store.

"The best part of the trip was seeing how Habitat for Humanity improves and changes people's lives," said participant Sarah Hall. The purpose of the trip was to serve a community and develop in faith, Sarah said.

Highlights, according to Sarah, were attending St. Patrick's Day Mass and parade in Savannah and staying overnight at St. Bernard Abbey in Cullman, Ala., which features the Ave Maria Grotto.

The partnership with the Newman Centers of the Diocese of Peoria began a few years ago when Father Radosevich had a vision of giving an opportunity to learn through service, missions and faith experiences, Sarah said.
