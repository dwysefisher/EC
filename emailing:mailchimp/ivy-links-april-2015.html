Faculty and staff are invited to welcome one of the most highly decorated veterans in Illinois to campus tomorrow night. Former Green Beret William Albracht of Rock Island will speak about a five-day siege to defend a U.S. Army outpost in the Vietnamese jungle during the Vietnam War at 7 p.m. in the Gammon Room in Melick Library. He will draw from his book "Abandoned in Hell" to recount the battle to defend Fire Base Kate in October 1969. The book was published by Penguin Press last month. A reception and book-signing will follow.

Associate professor of environmental studies Renée Mullen will present the final Clarence R. Noe Dean's Lecture of the year with "Keepin' Our Stormy Waters—With a Little Help From Our Friends" at 7:30 p.m. Thursday, April 9 in Becker Auditorium. She will discuss a storm water capture model Eureka College is developing to lessen the impacts of storm water run-off in Eureka and beyond. The landscaping of Sanders Hall will include deep-rooted native plants and their symbionts for capturing and cleansing storm water from the building. She also will describe a partnership with the city of Eureka and the importance of understanding soil communities and native plants for saving time, money and water as the city moves forward with its plans to upgrade its storm sewer system.

The Chorale and Chamber Singers will present their annual spring concert at 4 p.m. Sunday, April 12 in Becker Auditorium. Selections will include works by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Orazio Vecchi, Jean-Baptiste Weckerlin, Stephen Schwartz and Peter Lutkin and several folksong arrangements by Randol Alan Bass, Rollo Dilworth, John Miller, James Clemens and Jay Althouse. Senior piano performance major Darian Wohlrabe will perform the Rondo from Beethoven's "Piano Sonata No. 21 in C major, Op. 53," known as "The Waldstein."

Alumnus Joel Shoemaker will direct the regional premiere of the 2008 Pulitzer Prize-winning drama "August: Osage County" at 7:30 p.m. April 14-18 in Pritchard Theatre. The plot revolves around the disappearance of a family patriarch and the interplay of nasty recriminations, taunts and hurtful revelations that occur when relatives arrive, ostensibly to offer support to the wife. The production contains mature themes and adult language. Ticket prices are $10 for general admission and $7 for students. For reservations, e-mail or call the box office at Ext. 6363.
